# Issues

An issue is one fault on one lift. Every time something isn't working right — a sticky door, a strange noise, a lift gone offline — that's an issue.

## What you'll do

Most of the time, your day looks like this:

- Log issues when Building Managers call or email to report problems.
- Watch new issues come in from Technicians on-site.
- Close issues as they get fixed — automatically by a Job, or manually from the dashboard.

That's the whole flow. The rest of this page explains how each part works.

## The life of an issue

Every issue is either Open or Closed. You'll see these words on the screen.

| # | State | What it means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Open** | The issue has been raised and is still outstanding. It stays visible to your team and shows up automatically to any Technician who visits this lift. |
| 2 | **Closed** | The issue has been resolved or ruled out. It's saved to the lift's history with a reason. Closed issues can't be edited. |

## The three severity levels

Every issue carries one of three severity levels. Everyone on your team uses the same words for the same kind of problem, so triage stays consistent.

## How issues get opened

Issues can be raised in two ways. Both end up in the same place.

### By a Job

A Technician finds a fault on-site during a visit and logs it on the mobile app, against the lift they're standing in front of.

### By an Admin

You log it from the dashboard when a Building Manager calls or emails to report a problem.

## How issues get closed

Issues can be closed in two ways.

### By a Job (auto)

During a repair, the Technician marks the issue as **Resolved**. LiftAuth closes it automatically. If they mark it **Not Resolved**, the issue stays Open and rolls forward.

### By an Admin (manual)

From the dashboard, you can close any Open issue with a reason:

- **Resolved** — the fault is fixed.
- **Won't fix** — acknowledged, but no action will be taken.
- **Duplicate** — already tracked under another issue.
- **Decommissioned** — the lift has been taken out of service.

## A few important things

**Open issues follow the lift, not the person.** When a Technician starts a Job on a lift, every Open issue on that lift surfaces in their app — so known faults are addressed in the same visit, not forgotten.

**Severity is a triage signal, not a guarantee.** Severity tells your team what to do next. It doesn't change how the issue behaves in LiftAuth — a Critical issue and a Minor issue close the same way.

**Closed issues become part of the lift's history.** Once closed, an issue is saved against that lift forever, along with the close reason. If the same fault comes back, raise a new issue.
