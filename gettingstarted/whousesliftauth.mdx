LiftAuth is designed for three types of people. Understanding who does what will help you use the platform more confidently.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="briefcase">
    Office staff who manage the business from the **web dashboard** on a computer. They schedule jobs, review completed records, approve invoices, and manage the team.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Technician" icon="wrench">
    Field workers who visit buildings and service lifts. They use the **LiftAuth mobile app** on their phone to view jobs, complete checklists, and capture signatures on-site.\
  </Card>

  <Card title="Building Manager" icon="building">
    The person at the building who signs off on completed work. They receive a **link by email** — no account or login required.\
    \
    \
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Admin access levels 

Not all admins have the same access. There are two admin roles in LiftAuth:

<div style={{ marginTop: "-1rem", marginBottom: "-1rem" }}>
  | Role      | What they can do                                                   |
  | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
  | **Owner** | Full access — manage the team, billing, invoices, and all settings |
  | **Admin** | Manage jobs, invoices, and team members (cannot change billing)    |
</div>

<Tip>
  _Every company starts with one Owner. The Owner can then invite additional Admins and Technicians from the Team page._
</Tip>

## Technician access

Technicians only use the mobile app and can only see the jobs assigned to them. They cannot access the web dashboard, invoices, payments, or team settings.

<Warning>
  If a team member needs access to the web dashboard features, they must be invited as an **Admin** — not a Technician.
</Warning>
