A **maintenance plan** is the automation layer of LiftAuth. Once set up for a lift, it automatically creates a maintenance job and a draft invoice on a fixed basis — you just review and close them.

## What a maintenance plan does

Before setting up a plan, make sure you have already:

- [Added the lift](/admin/lifts)
- [Added the building manager](/admin/building-managers) and assigned them to the lift

## Creating a maintenance plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lift detail page">
    Go to **Lifts** and click on the lift you want to set up a plan for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create maintenance plan">
    Click the **Create maintenance plan** button. A setup guide will open — follow each step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save ">
    Click **Save**. The plan is now active. LiftAuth will automatically create and schedule jobs from here on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What is automated vs. what you still do manually

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="LiftAuth handles automatically" icon="circle-check">
    - Creating the maintenance job each cycle
    - Generating a draft invoice after the cycle closes
    - Sending the signing link to the Building Manager
  </Card>

  <Card title="You still need to do" icon="user">
    - Closing the completed job after it is signed
    - Reviewing and approving the draft invoice\
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Auto-created jobs will appear in the **Jobs** list. Just assign a technician and confirm the scheduled time — everything else flows automatically.
</Tip>

## Editing a plan

1. Open the lift's detail page.
2. Go to the **Maintenance Plans** tab and click on the plan.
3. Click **Edit**, make your changes, and save.

<Tip>
  Changes to a plan take effect from the next cycle — they will not affect jobs that have already been created.
</Tip>
