---

A **job** represents a service visit to a lift. Every time a technician goes to a site, there should be a job in LiftAuth to record it. Each job is tied to exactly one lift.

## The three job types

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card icon="calendar-check">
    ## Maintenance

    A planned, routine inspection. Usually created automatically by a Maintenance Plan. Use this for regular scheduled services.
  </Card>

  <Card icon="badge-x">
    ## Breakdown

    An emergency call-out. Use this when a lift has failed or is unsafe and needs immediate attention.
  </Card>

  <Card icon="badge-alert">
    ## Repair

    A visit to fix a specific known fault. Use this when you are following up on a reported issue.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Job statuses

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Scheduled** | Technician assigned and date/time confirmed |
| **Work Done** | Technician completed the job on-site. [Record](/admin/records) is created |
| **Signed** | Building Manager has signed the record |
| **Closed** | Admin has reviewed and closed the job |

## Creating a new job

<video src="/videos/result.mp4" controls />

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Jobs">
    Click **Jobs** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Schedule Job">
    Click the **Schedule Job** button and select the job type — Maintenance, Breakdown, or Repair.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the lift">
    Choose the lift that needs servicing. Each job covers one lift only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a technician">
    Select the technician who will carry out the work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the date and time">
    Enter the scheduled date and expected time window. Don't worry if you input this wrongly, you can [fix this after](/admin/jobs#rescheduling).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The technician will see this job in their mobile app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you have set up a [Maintenance Plan](/admin/maintenance-plans) for a lift, maintenance jobs are created automatically each cycle — you only need to assign the technician and confirm the time.
</Tip>

## Closing a completed job {#closing-a-job}

Once the technician has finished and the Building Manager has signed, the job will appear in your Inbox ready to close.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the job">
    Find it in the Inbox or in the Jobs list with status **Signed**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the record">
    Check the checklist results, photos, and signatures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the job">
    Click **Close job** and confirm. The job status changes to **Closed**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If the Building Manager was not on-site to sign, the technician can send them a signing link by email. The job will move to **Signed** once they complete it. Learn how in [Records](/admin/records#sending-a-signing-link).
</Tip>

## Rescheduling or cancelling a job {#rescheduling}

<Warning>
  Jobs can be rescheduled or cancelled, but cancelled jobs cannot be reopened. Create a new job if the visit still needs to happen.
</Warning>

1. Open the job from the Jobs list.
2. Click **Reschedule** or **Cancel job** and confirm.
