---

An **issue** is a fault or problem logged against a specific lift. Issues can be created by admins on the dashboard or by technicians on the mobile app while on-site. Once created, they stay open and visible until they are manually closed.

## Severity levels

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card icon="badge-x">
    ## Critical

    A safety risk that is preventing the lift from functioning right now.
  </Card>

  <Card icon="badge-info">
    ## Major

    Affects the lift's operation and is at risk of being out of service soon.
  </Card>

  <Card icon="badge-minus">
    ## Minor

    Cosmetic or low-impact. Can be addressed at the next scheduled visit.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Creating an issue

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Issues">
    Click **Issues** in the left sidebar, then click **New issue.**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the lift">
    Choose the lift the fault was found on. The issue will be linked to that lift's record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Enter a title, description, and severity level. You can also attach photos. 
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The issue is now open and linked to the lift.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also create an issue directly from a lift's detail page — open the lift, go to the Issues tab, and click **New issue**.
</Tip>

## How do Technicians sees issues

When a technician starts a job on a lift, any open issues linked to that lift are shown to them automatically. This gives them visibility of known faults before they begin work, so nothing is missed on-site.

## Editing an issue

1. Open the issue.
2. Click **Edit**.
3. Update the title, description, or severity and save.

<Warning>
  Closed issues cannot be edited.
</Warning>

## Closing an issue

Issues can be closed in two ways:

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="By a Job" icon="wrench">
    When a technician completes a repair job and marks an issue as **Resolved**, LiftAuth closes it automatically. If the technician marks it as **Not Resolved** (could not fix it this visit), the issue stays open.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Manually" icon="user">
    An admin can close an issue at any time from the dashboard.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the issue">
        Find it in the Issues list and click it.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Close issue">
        Click the **Close issue** button on the right side of the page.

        <Frame>
          ![4809fb0c 6326 47b8 A698 7a5e66899683](/images/4809fb0c-6326-47b8-a698-7a5e66899683.jpg)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select a close reason">
        - **Resolved** — the fault has been fixed
        - **Won't fix** — acknowledged but no action will be taken
        - **Duplicate** — already recorded under another issue
        - **Decommissioned** — the lift has been taken out of service
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
