---

A **lift** in LiftAuth represents one physical elevator unit at a building. Every lift must be registered through the **BCA LEAP portal** — this is how LiftAuth pulls the lift's official equipment details automatically.

## What is the BCA LEAP portal?

The [BCA (Building and Construction Authority) LEAP portal](https://www2.bca.gov.sg/leap/PTOEnquiry/Search) is the Singapore government's database of all registered lifts. Every lift has its own page on this portal with its equipment ID, address, and maintenance details.

To register a lift in LiftAuth, you need the URL of that lift's page on the BCA LEAP portal. It looks like this:

[https://www2.bca.gov.sg/leap/ptoenquiry/xxxxxxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx](https://www2.bca.gov.sg/leap/ptoenquiry/xxxxxxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx)

<Tip>
  You can also register a lift by scanning the QR code on the lift's equipment plate using the LiftAuth mobile app — this retrieves the same BCA URL automatically.
</Tip>

## Adding a new lift

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the lift on BCA LEAP">
    Go to the [BCA LEAP portal](https://www2.bca.gov.sg/leap/PTOEnquiry/Search), search for the lift, and open its PTO Enquiry page. Copy the full URL from your browser's address bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Lifts in LiftAuth">
    Click **Lifts** in the left sidebar, then click **Add lift**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the BCA URL">
    Paste the URL you copied into the BCA PTO Enquiry URL field and click **Look up**. LiftAuth will automatically pull the equipment ID, address, and other details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the remaining details">
    Confirm or correct the pre-filled details, select the lift type, and assign a **Building Manager** from the dropdown.

    <Tip>
      You can skip the Building Manager for now and assign one later from the lift's detail page — but note that a Building Manager must be assigned before you can create jobs or maintenance plans for the lift.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register the lift">
    Click **Register lift**. The lift is now in your system.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If a lift is already registered in your account, LiftAuth will notify you and prevent a duplicate from being created.
</Warning>
