# Maintenance Plans

A maintenance plan is a recurring schedule for one lift. Once you set it up, LiftAuth creates the Maintenance Jobs and drafts the invoices for you, every cycle.

## What you'll do

Most of the time, your day looks like this:

- Set up a plan once per lift, when you take on a new contract.
- Review and close each Maintenance Job after the Technician has finished and the Building Manager has signed.
- Approve the draft invoice LiftAuth creates at the end of each cycle.

That's the whole flow. The rest of this page explains how each part works.

## The three parts of a plan

A maintenance plan is just three things. You set these once, and LiftAuth uses them every cycle.

| # | Part | What it means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Primary Technician** | The Technician who usually services this lift. LiftAuth assigns them to every Maintenance Job the plan creates. |
| 2 | **Preferred Schedule** | How often you visit — monthly, quarterly |
| 3 | **Invoicing Template** | What you'll bill the Building Manager each cycle — the amount, the line items, and the payment terms. |

## What happens each cycle

Once a plan is active, this is what LiftAuth does for you on every cycle, with no input from your team:

1. **Schedules a Maintenance Job** **14 days before**, with the Primary Technician already assigned and the scheduled date set from the preferred schedule.
2. **Surfaces the Job in the Technician's app** so they can pick it up on the day.
3. **Drafts an invoice** when the cycle closes, built from the invoicing template.
4. **Lands the draft in your Inbox** for review and approval.

## What you still do

LiftAuth handles the repetition. You handle the judgment.

- **Set up the plan**, once per lift.
- **Review the completed record** before closing the Job — make sure the Technician's checklist and photos look right.
- **Review and approve the draft invoice** before it goes to the Building Manager.

## A few important things

**One active plan per lift.** A lift can only have one Maintenance Plan running at a time. To change the frequency or the fee, edit the plan — don't create a second one.

**Draft invoices wait for you.** LiftAuth creates the draft and parks it in your Inbox. Nothing is sent to the Building Manager until you approve it.

**The Building Manager doesn't see the plan.** Plans are internal to your team. From the Building Manager's side, all they see is the regular Maintenance Jobs and the invoices once you've approved them.
