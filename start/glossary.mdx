Use this page as a reference whenever you come across an unfamiliar word in LiftAuth.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Lift">
    The physical elevator unit at a building. Each lift has its own record in LiftAuth with details like location, type, manufacturer, and serial number.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Building Manager">
    The company or person responsible for the building where the lift is located. They receive invoices and sign off on completed work.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tenant / Team">
    The company or team that uses LiftAuth to manage and maintain lifts. Everything in LiftAuth — lifts, jobs, invoices, and team members — belongs to a Tenant. If you work for one company, you will only ever see one Tenant.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Job">
    A service visit to a lift. Every time a technician goes to a site, it is recorded as a job. Jobs can be Maintenance, Breakdown, or Repair.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Maintenance Job">
    A planned, routine service — for example, a monthly or quarterly inspection. Usually created automatically by a Maintenance Plan.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Breakdown Job">
    An emergency call-out when the lift has stopped working or is unsafe to use.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Repair Job">
    A visit to fix a specific, known fault that was previously reported.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Checklist">
    A list of items the technician must inspect during a maintenance visit. Each item is marked as Pass or Fail.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Record / Jobsheet">
    The written report created when a technician completes a job. It includes checklist results, photos, notes, and signatures. It can be downloaded as a PDF.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Signature">
    The sign-off that confirms a job was completed. Both the technician and the building manager must sign each jobsheet.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Issue / Fault">
    A problem found on a lift. Issues are given a severity level — Critical, Major, or Minor — and tracked until they are resolved.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Invoice">
    The bill sent to the building manager after work is completed. Invoices are created automatically and must be reviewed by an admin before they are sent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Maintenance Plan">
    A schedule that tells LiftAuth how often a lift needs a service (e.g. every month). Once set up, LiftAuth automatically creates jobs and invoices each cycle.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="BCA Equipment ID">
    A unique code assigned to a lift under Australian building compliance regulations. LiftAuth can look up lift details automatically using this code.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="OTP (One-Time Password)">
    A 6-digit code sent to your phone by SMS or to your email. You use it to log in instead of a traditional password. It expires after a few minutes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Draft Invoice">
    An invoice that has been automatically generated but not yet sent. An admin must review and approve it before it goes to the building manager.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PDF">
    A downloadable document version of a completed jobsheet, including all checklist results, photos, and signatures.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
