# Jobs

A job is one piece of work on one lift. Every job belongs to a specific lift, has a type, and goes through the same steps from start to finish. Once it's closed, it becomes part of the lift's permanent history.

## What a job is

A job is a unit of work on a specific lift. Each job:

- Belongs to one lift.
- Has a type — **Maintenance**, **Breakdown**, or **Repair**.
- Goes through the same steps as every other job.
- Is assigned to a technician once it's scheduled.
- Becomes part of the lift's history when it's closed.

A cancelled job is not saved to the lift's history.

## How a job moves

A job moves through five steps:

**Open → Scheduled → Work Done → Signed → Closed**

| Step | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Open | Created, but no technician or date yet. |
| Scheduled | A technician and time are set. Ready to be worked. |
| Work Done | The technician has finished on-site. Waiting for the customer to sign. |
| Signed | The building rep has signed the jobsheet. |
| Closed | Admin has reviewed and closed the job. It's now part of the lift's history. |
| Cancelled | The job won't happen. Not saved to history. |

Most jobs barely sit in **Open**. A maintenance job from the plan is opened and scheduled in one step. A breakdown job created on-site is opened and scheduled on the technician immediately. **Open** is what you see when admin has created a job but hasn't picked a technician or date yet.

A job can be **cancelled** at any point before it's closed — even after the customer has signed, if admin needs to void it. **Closed** and **Cancelled** are final.

Signing always requires a signature from the building rep. Admin can't sign on the customer's behalf.

If something needs redoing, admin can step a job back one stage — for example from **Signed** back to **Work Done**.

## The five-step workflow

Every job type follows the same five steps:

1. The job is created and assigned to a technician — it shows as **Scheduled**.
2. The technician attends on-site and does the work.
3. The technician marks the job **Work Done**.
4. The building rep signs the jobsheet — the job becomes **Signed**.
5. Admin reviews and closes the job — it becomes **Closed** and is saved to the lift's history.

What changes between types is **how the job is created** and **what the technician captures on-site**.

## Types of job

### Maintenance

Routine recurring service. The technician works through a checklist on-site — each item passed or failed, with notes and photos where needed.

Maintenance jobs are usually created automatically by the lift's maintenance plan. The plan sets the cadence — **monthly** or **quarterly** — plus a preferred day and time window. Admin can also create one manually for ad-hoc service.

### Breakdown

An unplanned visit to a faulty lift, triggered when a building manager or occupant reports a fault. Free-form notes and photos rather than a checklist.

There are two ways a breakdown job gets created:

- **Technician on-site** — the technician arrives at the lift and creates the job on themselves. They don't wait for admin.
- **Admin dispatch** — admin creates the job in the dashboard and sends a technician.

If the fault can't be fixed on the spot, admin usually creates a separate repair job as a follow-up. That's a manual step — breakdowns don't auto-create repairs.

### Repair

A scheduled visit to fix something found earlier, usually during a breakdown, sometimes during maintenance. Notes and photos describe the work done.

Repair jobs are usually scheduled by admin, especially when parts need to be ordered. A technician can also create one from the app.
