# Jobs

A job is one piece of work on one lift. Every time a technician goes out to a lift — for routine service, a breakdown, or a repair — that visit is a job.

## What you'll do

Most of the time, your day looks like this:

- Create jobs and send technicians to lifts.
- Watch jobs come in as technicians finish them.
- Check the work and close the job once the customer has signed.

That's the whole flow. The rest of this page explains how each part works.

## The life of a job

Every job goes through the same four stages. You'll see these words on the screen, so it helps to know what each one means.

| # | Stage | What it means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Scheduled** | A Technician is assigned and a date is set. |
| 2 | **Work Done** | The Technician has finished on-site. The Job is now waiting for the Building Manager to sign. |
| 3 | **Signed** | The Building Manager has signed the jobsheet on the technician's phone. |
| 4 | **Closed** | You've checked the job and closed it. It's now saved to the lift's history. |

## The three kinds of job

There are three kinds of job. They all follow the same stages above. The difference is what the Technician does and what kind of Record is produced.

## Maintenance Job

Routine service visits — the regular check-ups you do on every lift. This is often scheduled by a Maintenance Plan. A Maintenance Record is created.

## Breakdown Job

The Building Manager reports a breakdown. A Technician is dispatched immediately, diagnoses the issue, and creates the breakdown job on-site. A Breakdown Record is created.

## Repair Job

Once parts are procured, admin schedules a repair job. The technician visits the site, does the repair, and resolves any outstanding issues. a Repair Job is created.

## A few important things

**Only the Building Manager can sign for themselves.** You can't sign for them from the dashboard. The signature has to be done on the technician's phone, on-site, or signed remotely by them.

**Closed jobs become part of the lift's history.** Once you close a job, it's saved against that lift forever. A cancelled job isn't saved.
